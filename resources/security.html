Staying Safe Online

As technology gets smarter, scams get smarter too. Knowing the basics can protect your identity, money, and peace of mind.

Use Strong Passwords

Weak passwords are still one of the biggest risks online. Use long, unique passwords for every account.

  • Use 12+ characters
  • Mix letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Never reuse passwords
  • Use a password manager

Turn on Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds a second layer of protection. Even if someone gets your password, they still need your device.

Watch for Phishing

Scammers often pretend to be banks, delivery services, or government agencies.

  • Check sender email carefully
  • Never click suspicious links
  • Be cautious with urgent language
  • Verify before responding

Protect Your Devices

  • Keep software updated
  • Use antivirus protection
  • Lock your devices
  • Back up important files

AI Safety Matters Too

AI tools are powerful, but avoid sharing:

  • Passwords
  • Personal health information
  • Financial details
  • Private business data
